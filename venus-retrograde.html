What Venus retrograde actually means

About every 18 months, Venus slows, stops, and appears to move backward for around 40 days, an optical effect of Earth overtaking her in a faster, tighter orbit. Venus governs love and attraction, money and possessions, beauty and taste, so when she turns back, all of it turns inward for review. It is one of the rarer retrogrades: unlike Mercury's three-times-a-year cadence, Venus does this only once every 18 months or so, and sometimes, like 2027, not at all in a given calendar year.

The plain experience is a re-weighing. Old partners resurface, sometimes literally, sometimes just in memory. Purchases you were sure about get second-guessed. A haircut or a big style change you were about to make suddenly feels like it should wait. None of this is punishment: Venus is asking what you actually value, as opposed to what you have simply gotten used to wanting.

What to hold, and what to lean into

Hold, where you can: a new relationship's first steps, an engagement or wedding date, a major cosmetic change, and any large purchase meant to hold its value, a ring, a home, a piece of art. Traditionally you wait for Venus direct to make these carry their full weight cleanly. What does Venus retrograde mean?

About every 18 months, Venus appears to move backward for roughly 40 days. Venus governs love, beauty, money, and what you value, so the season tends to bring old partners back into view, second-guessed purchases, and a general re-weighing of what you actually want versus what you settled for.

What should you avoid during Venus retrograde?

Traditionally: starting a brand-new relationship, getting engaged or married, a major cosmetic change like a big haircut or a tattoo, and a large purchase of something meant to hold its value, like a ring or a home. None of these are forbidden, they simply tend to be revisited once Venus goes direct.

Is Venus retrograde bad for relationships already together?

No, and it can be quietly good. Existing relationships often deepen: old wounds surface to actually be healed, not just felt again, and the season rewards honest conversation over avoidance. It favors revisiting and repairing far more than it threatens what already works.
