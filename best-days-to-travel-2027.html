How a travel date is chosen

Long journeys traditionally belong to the 9th house, and the Moon carries the change itself, so a travel election reads much like our relocation timing: distance and movement, just at trip scale rather than a permanent move. The strongest days want the Moon in a cardinal or mutable sign (Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn; or Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces), the signs of initiation and motion, rather than a fixed sign, which favors staying put. From there, the Moon should be applying to Venus or Jupiter, ease and good fortune on the road, and clear of the void of course, the classic warning that a trip begun then goes sideways.

What this page is best for

This suits the start of a real journey: a trip you have planned, a departure day that matters. It is less suited to routine travel, the commute or the weekly business flight, where timing this closely stops being useful. If your trip carries real weight, a wedding abroad, a long-awaited return, a move disguised as a visit, the same care applied to our other elections applies here too.

How is a travel date chosen astrologically?

The 9th house rules long journeys and foreign places, and the Moon rules the change itself. A good travel election wants a waxing Moon in a cardinal or mutable sign, the signs that favor movement over the fixed signs' staying-put nature, applying to Venus or Jupiter and clear of the void of course.

Does this apply to any kind of trip?

This reads the same election as our relocation timing: the significators for a trip and a move are the same, distance and change, just at different scales. It suits the start of a real journey more than routine travel, a business trip you have taken a dozen times, or a quick weekend a few miles away.

What should I avoid when picking a travel date?

A void-of-course Moon at departure, traditionally a sign the trip will not go as planned, delays or a change of course. Every date on this page already excludes it. Mercury retrograde is also worth avoiding for travel involving contracts, tickets, or itineraries that could get crossed up.
