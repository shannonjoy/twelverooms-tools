The twelve houses, read as rooms

Every chart has twelve houses, and houses are the most honest metaphor astrology owns: rooms are things you live in. Nobody lives in one room. This is the tour, room by room, in the whole-sign system: what each one holds, and how to find yours. It is also, yes, where this practice got its name.

How houses work, in one minute

Your rising sign sets the floor plan. In whole-sign houses, the oldest system in Western astrology, the sign rising in the east at your birth becomes your entire first house, the next sign your second, and so on around the wheel. Three things furnish each room: planets in the house bring their voices into that area of life; the sign on the house sets the room's style; and the house's ruling planet, wherever it sits in your chart, is the landlord: where that room's business actually gets decided. An empty room still has a landlord. That one idea, followed honestly, is most of house reading.

I
The First House · the front door
Body, temperament, the way you enter every other room. This is the room where you meet the world: your face, your instinctive style, the first read people get. The whole chart is answered from here, which is why the rising sign matters so much: it is not one detail among many, it is the floor plan's anchor.
II
The Second House · the pantry
Money, possessions, skills, and the quieter question underneath them: what you consider yours, and what you consider yourself worth. A stocked pantry is not greed, it is provision. This room shows how you earn, keep, and value, and its landlord shows where your livelihood actually comes from.
III
The Third House · the writing desk
Mind in motion: speaking, writing, learning, errands, siblings, neighbors, the daily rounds. This is the room of the near world, everything within a short trip of home. A strong third house lives by the word: charts with the tenth-house ruler here often build a career on writing and speaking.
IV
The Fourth House · the hearth
Home, family, parents, roots, and the private ground floor of the life. The fourth sits at the bottom of the chart the way a foundation sits under a house. It holds where you come from and where you land at day's end, and late in life, what you retire into. Its condition says what home needs to feel like for you.
V
The Fifth House · the music room
Pleasure, play, romance, children, creative work: everything made for the joy of making it. This is the room where the life stops being maintenance and starts being lived. Its planets show how you delight; its landlord shows where your delight tends to happen.
VI
The Sixth House · the workroom
Daily work, routines, health, service, the body's maintenance and the calendar's. Not the career (that is the tenth): the work itself, the practice, the craft repeated until it is yours. A tended sixth house is why some lives run smoothly: someone is doing the unglamorous work of upkeep, and it is you.
VII
The Seventh House · the table set for two
Marriage, partnership, close collaborators, open rivals: the room where the important others sit down across from you. Directly opposite the first house, it is the life lived at eye level with someone else. Its sign describes who you partner with and how; its landlord, where partnership finds you.
VIII
The Eighth House · the vault
Shared resources, debts, inheritance, intimacy, mortality, and everything a life would rather not discuss at dinner. The old texts were wary of this room; the honest reading is that it holds what is entangled with others and what must eventually be faced. Charts strong here handle what most people look away from.
IX
The Ninth House · the library
Belief, higher learning, long journeys, philosophy, publishing, the law: the room with the maps and the far windows. The third house is the near world; the ninth is the far one, everything that requires leaving what you know. Its planets show how you seek meaning; its landlord, where you find it.
X
The Tenth House · the room with your name on the door
Career, reputation, public standing, the visible work of the life. The tenth sits at the top of the chart, in full daylight: what you are known for, what you build in view of others. Where its ruler sits is one of the most useful single facts in a chart: it shows which room the career actually runs through.
XI
The Eleventh House · the gathering room
Friends, allies, community, audiences, and the hopes that need other people to come true. The tenth is what you build; the eleventh is who stands with you while you build it, and who benefits after. Strong eleventh houses are carried by their people, and carry them back.
XII
The Twelfth House · the quiet room at the back
Solitude, sleep, the unconscious, hidden work, endings, and what is carried privately. Every house needs one room the guests never see. The twelfth holds what restores you away from witnesses and what undoes you when ignored. Planets here work behind the scenes: honored, they are the deepest room in the house.

Reading your own rooms

Start with your free natal chart: with a birth time, it lays all twelve houses out from your rising sign. Then walk the wheel counterclockwise, first house first, and for any room that matters to you right now, ask the three questions: which planets are in it, which sign styles it, and where its landlord went. The landlord question is the one template reports skip, and it is where house reading becomes chart reading.

And if you only remember one thing: an empty house is not an empty life. Most charts leave half the rooms unoccupied. The sign is still on the door, the landlord is still somewhere in the building, and the room still gets lived in: it simply gets managed from another part of the house.

Common questions

What are the 12 houses?

Twelve areas of life, laid out from your rising sign: self, money, mind, home, pleasure, work, partnership, depth, belief, career, community, and the inner life. Together they are the whole floor plan of a life.

What are whole-sign houses?

The oldest house system: your rising sign is your entire first house, the next sign your second, and so on. One sign, one house, no split rooms. It is the system this site and every Twelve Rooms reading uses.

What does an empty house mean?

Nothing is missing. The sign on the house has a ruling planet somewhere in your chart, and that planet, the landlord, shows where the empty room's business is handled. Every house has a landlord.

Which house is most important?

The first, because it anchors the floor plan, and the tenth, because it is the most visible. But the honest answer is whichever room your chart's planets and current transits are lighting up: importance moves. That is what a timed reading is for.

Do I need my birth time?

For houses, yes. They are set by your rising sign, which changes about every two hours. No time means no houses: you still have a true chart of planets in signs, just not the architecture.
