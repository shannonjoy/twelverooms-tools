How a surgery election is chosen, traditionally

Surgery is Mars's domain, the blade itself, so the tradition treats him as necessary but dangerous: present in the chart by virtue of the act, but never strong or angular, never in a hard contact with the Moon. The Moon herself is the body and the patient's own flow through the procedure, so she should be supported by Jupiter (healing, resilience) and Venus (ease, gentleness), and away from an uncontrolled Moon-Mars contact (hemorrhage, in the old language) and Moon-Saturn (complications, a hard recovery). Unlike almost every other election here, surgery wants a waning Moon, since the work is to remove or reduce, not to grow.

The rule this page cannot apply

The single most load-bearing traditional rule is body-part specific: never operate with the Moon in the sign that rules the part of the body being treated. Aries rules the head, Taurus the neck and throat, Gemini the arms and lungs, Cancer the chest and stomach, Leo the heart and back, Virgo the belly and gut, Libra the kidneys and lower back, Scorpio the pelvis and reproductive organs, Sagittarius the hips and thighs, Capricorn the knees and bones, Aquarius the calves and ankles, Pisces the feet. This page scores the sky in general, not for a named procedure, so it cannot check your date against your own body part. That check is yours, or your reader's, to make by hand, every time.
