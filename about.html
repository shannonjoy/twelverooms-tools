A natal chart is not a verdict

It is a seed: everything you could become, planted the moment you arrived. Transits are the seasons that call it up, year by year, and you are the one growing. The Twelve Rooms reads the seed with real precision and reads the seasons with warmth, so you can live your chart on purpose.

Why "The Twelve Rooms"

Every chart has twelve houses, and houses are the most honest metaphor astrology owns: rooms are things you live in. Your first house is the room where you meet the world, your seventh the room where the important others sit down across from you, your tenth the room with your name on the door. A reading here is a tour of the whole house, every room entered, every room's landlord found, even the ones that look empty.

The method

Whole-sign houses, the system Hellenistic astrology was built on, where each house is one whole sign counted from your rising. Sect-aware, meaning your chart is read differently by day than by night, the oldest sorting question in the tradition and the one template reports skip. Computed, never approximated: every position comes from the Swiss Ephemeris, the same astronomical engine professional software uses, from your exact birth date, time, and place. Your birth time matters here: it sets the rising sign, the Midheaven, and all twelve rooms.

And then the part no engine can do: the whole chart, read together. One cohesive reading in which your Sun section knows what your Moon section said. I read every line myself.

What astrology is for

Planning, reflection, self-understanding. Never doom, never fortune-telling, never "the universe has plans." The chart is a map of seasons, not a script; you are always the protagonist. That belief shapes everything here, from the readings to the free tools: honest language about what a transit can and cannot say, beginnings timed to clear skies where you can choose them, and a firm refusal to sell fear.

The free tools, and your privacy

The natal chart, Moon page, synastry grid, and timing finder are free, with no account required. Your birth data is computed in memory and never stored, logged, or shared. The site uses Google Analytics to see which pages people read and roughly where they come from, so the tools can get better; it never receives your birth details. The tools' source code is public under the AGPL. That is the practice's posture: precision you can check, and a chart that stays yours.
