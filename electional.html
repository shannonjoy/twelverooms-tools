How to choose your moment

Electional astrology turns the usual practice around. Instead of reading a chart you were handed, you choose the moment, so the thing you begin is born under a sky you picked on purpose. Astrologers have set wedding dates, business launches, and signings this way for centuries, because a beginning carries the weather of the hour it starts in.

The Moon leads every election. She is the fastest body and she carries the work forward, so the first rule is to keep her out of the void, that coasting gap before she changes sign when beginnings tend to drift. From there you want her applying to a benefic, moving toward an easy aspect with Venus or Jupiter, and away from the harder contacts. The finder above does exactly this: it drops the void, scores the Moon's aspects, and hands you the best windows with the reasons shown, so you are never trusting a black box.

Around her, the supporting cast. A planetary hour ruled by a friendly planet, a benefic near the angles, and, for anything built on words or contracts, Mercury moving direct rather than retrograde. This is not a horoscope, which reads a moment already given. It is the opposite: a way to find the good moment before it arrives, and to walk into it ready.

One honest note. This free tool reads the sky itself, which is plenty for many choices. For a wedding or a launch that truly matters, the strongest window is the one that also speaks well to your own birth chart, and that is the deeper work a full electional reading is for.
