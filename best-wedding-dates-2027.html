How these dates are chosen

In electional astrology you choose the birth chart of the marriage, and the Moon leads every election. For a wedding she should be waxing, so the union grows in light, and not void of course, the coasting gap where beginnings drift. She should be applying to a benefic, moving toward a trine, sextile, or conjunction with Venus (love) or Jupiter (blessing and abundance), and away from Mars (strife) and Saturn (coldness). Venus and Mercury should be direct, Venus because she rules the marriage and Mercury because he rules the vows and the paperwork. The strongest days of the year add one more grace: a Moon in Taurus, her sign of exaltation, which is why April 7, November 12, and December 9 rise above the rest.

What to avoid in 2027

The three Mercury retrogrades: Feb 10 to Mar 4, Jun 11 to Jul 5, and Oct 8 to 29. Signing vows and contracts under a backward Mercury invites revisited terms and crossed wires, so the dates above steer clear of all three. See the full Mercury retrograde 2027 guide.

The eclipse seasons: a solar eclipse on Feb 6 and lunar on Feb 20, then a cluster around Jul 18, Aug 2, and Aug 17. Astrologers keep weddings a good several days clear of eclipses, whose energy is abrupt and fated. That makes February and the back half of summer the year's weakest windows for a wedding, even where the Moon's best available day looks fine on paper.
