How to read the Moon's day

The Moon is the quick hand on the sky's clock. She moves about thirteen degrees a day and changes sign every two to three days, so where she sits is the emotional weather of the moment, the mood underneath the hours. Every timing tradition starts with her for exactly this reason: she is the fastest thing moving, and she touches everything.

When the Moon is void of course, she has made her last major aspect in a sign and is coasting toward the next one, forming no new connections along the way. The old counsel is simple: do not begin what you need to go somewhere. A launch, a signature, a first date started in the void tends to drift or quietly come to nothing. It is not a bad omen, it is a pause. Rest in it, tidy, reflect, finish what is already moving, and save the beginnings for when she has crossed into a fresh sign.

Her phase is the other half of the read. A waxing Moon, from new toward full, favors building and starting and gathering. A waning Moon, from full back toward new, favors releasing, editing, and letting go. And when she enters a new sign, the tone turns over: the same day can feel one way under a Cancer Moon and another entirely once she steps into Leo.

None of this decides your day for you. It is a map of the seasons in miniature, one turn of the wheel, so you can move with the current instead of against it.

Common questions

What sign is the Moon in today?

The Moon changes sign every two to three days, touring all twelve signs in about a month. The reading at the top of this page shows her sign, degree, and phase right now, computed live with the Swiss Ephemeris in your own timezone.

What does void of course mean?

The Moon is void of course after she makes her last major aspect to another planet and before she enters the next sign. During that gap she is forming no new connections, so beginnings started then tend to drift. It is a pause, not a curse.

Is a void of course Moon bad?

No. A void Moon is a poor time to launch, sign, or decide something you want to go somewhere, because it tends to fizzle. It is a fine time to rest, tidy, reflect, and finish what is already underway. Work with the pause rather than against it.

How often does the Moon change signs?

Roughly every two to two and a half days. The Moon moves about thirteen degrees a day, faster than any other body, which is why she sets the emotional weather of the day and why timing work always starts with her.
