What Mercury retrograde actually means

Three or four times a year, Mercury appears from Earth to slow, stop, and move backward for about three weeks. It is an optical effect of two planets orbiting at different speeds, not Mercury truly reversing, but the symbolism is old and consistent. Mercury governs the mind and the tongue, contracts and messages, short travel and the small machines we run our lives on. When it turns back, all of that turns inward for a review.

The plain experience is delay and doubling. Messages cross, plans change, the file you needed goes missing, the trip reroutes, the old friend resurfaces. None of it is punishment. The season simply favors the second pass over the first: anything with the prefix re goes well, and anything brand new goes better if you wait.

What to do, and what to hold

Lean in: revise the draft, review the numbers, reconnect with someone, repair the thing you let slide, return to the project you shelved. Back up your files before it begins. Reread contracts already on the table. Mercury retrograde is a gift to editors, menders, and anyone finishing rather than starting.

Hold, where you can: signing a contract, launching something you want to run smoothly, buying electronics or a car, and any first-time commitment that matters. These are beginnings, and a beginning carries the weather of its start. A launch made under a clear Mercury simply has an easier road. If you cannot wait, read every line twice and plan to revisit.

What does Mercury retrograde mean?

Three or four times a year Mercury appears to move backward from our view for about three weeks. Mercury governs thinking, speaking, contracts, travel, and technology, so the period reads as a review: things already in motion snag, and things done twice go smoother than things done new.

Is Mercury retrograde bad?

No. It is a season, not a curse. It favors anything with the prefix re: revise, review, reconnect, repair, return. It simply asks you not to start fresh commitments you want to run smoothly, and to back up your files and reread the fine print.
