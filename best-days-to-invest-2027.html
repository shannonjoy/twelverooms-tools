The rules of financial timing, traditionally

Financial or mundane astrology is an old branch, and its electional rules are consistent. They are timing lore, a way to begin with intention, not a forecast of what a market will do.

Jupiter is the planet of wealth, growth, and good fortune, the one you want strong and well-placed. Venus is value and worth. Mercury rules commerce, the exchange itself, and the paperwork, and the cardinal rule is that Mercury should be direct, never retrograde, when you transact or sign. The Moon is the timer, as always: she should be waxing to build and increase, not void of course (a venture begun void is said to come to nothing), and applying to a benefic (Jupiter or Venus) rather than to Mars, which brings loss and volatility, or Saturn, which brings decline and restriction. The 5th house is the traditional house of speculation and risk, the 2nd is your own money, and the 8th is other people's. For the day and hour, tradition favors Mercury (Wednesday, commerce) and Jupiter (Thursday, gain).

What this is, and what it is not

This is a way to choose a considered, symbolic starting point for a financial habit, the same way you might choose a wedding date. It is not a signal to buy or sell, not a prediction of price or return, and not a substitute for research or professional advice. Markets move on economics, not planets. Anyone who tells you the stars will make you money is selling you something, and it is not us.

Common questions

Can astrology tell you the best day to invest?

Traditional electional astrology can suggest an auspicious day to begin something, such as opening an account or starting a long-term investing habit, using significators like Jupiter for growth and a well-placed Moon. It does not predict stock prices, market direction, or returns, and it is not financial advice. Astrology does not move markets.

What does financial astrology consider?

Jupiter for wealth and expansion, Venus for value, Mercury for the transaction and paperwork (and Mercury should be direct, not retrograde), and the Moon as the timer: waxing to build, not void of course, applying to a benefic rather than to Mars or Saturn. The 5th house is the traditional house of speculation.

Should you avoid Mercury retrograde for financial decisions?

Traditionally, yes, for signing and transacting: Mercury rules commerce and contracts, so paperwork done while it is retrograde is prone to errors and revisions. In 2027 Mercury is retrograde Feb 10 to Mar 4, Jun 11 to Jul 5, and Oct 8 to 29. This is timing lore, not a market forecast.
