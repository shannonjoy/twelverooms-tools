How these dates are chosen

Legal timing is the most adversarial election in the traditional list, closer to horary practice than a simple beginning. You are read as the Ascendant and the Moon. The opponent is the 7th house, the house of open enemies and the other party. The judge and justice are Jupiter and the 9th house. The lawyers are Mercury. The conflict itself is Mars, which you want controlled, not eliminated; a court case needs some Mars to have teeth, but not so much that it runs the show. A strong filing date wants the Moon applying to Jupiter or the Sun, favor and authority, away from Saturn and Mars afflicting your side, with Mercury direct so the paperwork and arguments hold.

What to avoid in 2027

The three Mercury retrogrades: Feb 10 to Mar 4, Jun 11 to Jul 5, and Oct 8 to 29, excluded from every date above since Mercury governs your lawyers and your filings. The eclipse seasons, around Feb 6 and 20 and around Jul 18, Aug 2, and Aug 17, are unstable ground for anything adversarial; two of the table's Good months, July and August, sit close enough to an eclipse that padding the date by a few extra days is worth considering.

Why this one wants the full reading

More than any other election on this site, legal timing is genuinely two-sided: the sky above can look strong for you and equally strong for whoever you are up against, since the tool has no way to weigh a specific opponent's own chart. The single best date is the one where your significators read stronger than theirs, which only a full electional reading, done by hand against both sides, can actually judge.

Does the day I file actually matter legally?

No, none of this affects the legal merits, deadlines, or procedure of your case, which your attorney handles. This is the older, symbolic practice of choosing a favorable sky to begin an undertaking, alongside the legal work, never instead of it.
