How to read your timeline

A transit is simply where a planet is right now, meeting where it stood on the day you were born. The fast planets move too quickly to matter for long; the story is in the slow ones. Jupiter (about twelve years to circle the zodiac), Saturn (about twenty-nine), and the outer planets Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto, which can spend years on a single degree. When one of them makes an exact aspect to a planet in your chart, it opens a chapter you can feel.

Jupiter brings growth and opportunity, and it comes often, so treat its aspects as favorable weather rather than landmarks. Saturn brings structure and tests; its hard aspects are the ones that ask you to grow up a little, and its return near thirty and again near sixty is a genuine threshold. Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto are rare and deep, so when one contacts your Sun, Moon, or angles, that is a multi-year turning point worth planning around. The timeline flags those.

Because a planet can retrograde back and forth over the same degree, a single transit often has two or three exact passes across a year. The whole window is the season; the dates are its high tides.

Common questions

What is a transit?

Where the planets are now, meeting where they were at your birth. When a slow planet makes an exact aspect to one of your natal planets, it marks a chapter: a growth window, a test, or a turning point.

Do I need my birth time?

Not for transits to your planets, which a birth date pins closely. You do need it for transits to your Ascendant and Midheaven, and for precise Moon dates, since those depend on the exact minute.

What is a Saturn return?

When Saturn comes back to where it sat at your birth, near ages 29 and 58. It marks a threshold into a more grounded, self-authored chapter. Jupiter returns more often, about every 12 years, opening a new cycle of growth.

How is this calculated?

Swiss Ephemeris, whole-sign houses, the slow planets scanned day by day for their exact aspects to your chart, retrograde passes included. Your details are computed in memory and never stored, logged, or shared.
